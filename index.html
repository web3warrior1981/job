The Story Behind This Page

I have been building full-time in Web3 and technology for years — not as a side hustle, not for the hype, but as a true believer in what decentralised technology can do for the world. While I built, my wife worked full-time to keep our family afloat. She never once asked me to stop. She believed in what I was building even when nobody else did.

"Then, without warning, everything changed."

A real-life situation has arisen — sudden, serious, and deeply urgent. The kind that doesn't wait for product-market fit or the next funding round. The kind that forces a father to confront what he stands to lose if he doesn't act right now.

At the centre of everything is my son. ❤️ He is 7 and a half years old — extraordinary, complex, full of fight. He is autistic. He has ADHD. And he carries a genetic condition that overlaps with Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy, a rare and progressive muscle disorder that affects only a handful of children in the world.

He was born developing normally. By age three he could speak words, sing rhymes, light up a room. Then, gradually — word by word, sound by sound — he lost it all. He has regressed. Today he speaks only 4 to 5 words, and even those come hesitantly, like signals from a distant star. His CK levels — a clinical marker of muscle damage — range between 6,000 and 22,000. A healthy child's should be under 200. He is on steroids. He does physiotherapy twice a week. He has occupational therapy, speech therapy, a specialist school, a tutor — an entire ecosystem of care built carefully around him, thread by thread.

Every single one of those threads exists because we have not moved. Because I have fought every day to keep his world stable, predictable, and safe. That stability is now under serious threat.

I need to raise $50,000 urgently. This is the number that keeps my son's care intact, keeps my family together, and keeps a builder at his desk instead of watching everything fall apart. I am not here asking for sympathy. I am offering my best work — and trusting this community to meet me where I am.
